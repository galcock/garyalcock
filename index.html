Overview

I work at the intersection of private equity, technology, and precision physics. My background is in leveraged buyouts, corporate divestitures, and special situations across industrials, manufacturing, and technology-driven services. In parallel, I pursue independent research on a scalar-field framework for gravity and light, Density Field Dynamics (DFD), with concrete experimental proposals based on state-of-the-art optical clock systems.

I am based in Los Angeles and hold a B.S. in Corporate Finance from the University of Southern California.

Areas of work

Private equity and special situations

I have led and supported transactions across the full deal cycle: sourcing, diligence, structuring, and post-close value creation. Most of my work has been in industrials, manufacturing, and related services, including carve-outs and operationally intensive situations.

Previous roles include Managing Director positions at Liberty Bay Capital and Revolution Capital Group, and advisory work at Houlihan Lokey. I have been involved in more than 50 completed transactions with aggregate enterprise value in excess of $10 billion.

Density Field Dynamics and precision tests

My research centers on Density Field Dynamics (DFD), a scalar-refractive framework in which a field ψ(x) sets both the optical index and test-mass acceleration on flat ℝ³. The program is designed to be falsifiable with existing and near-term experiments.

Current work includes PPN analysis, strong-field and gravitational-wave phenomenology, and proposals for sector-resolved cavity–atom comparisons and optical clock tests of Local Position Invariance.

Optical clocks and LPI-style anomalies

I re-analyze long-baseline optical clock comparison data to examine whether photons and atoms respond identically to the same energy-density environment, and how to design experiments that cleanly test that question.

This includes work with ROCIT-style datasets and proposed height-separated cavity–atom configurations that would isolate sector-dependent couplings beyond General Relativity.

Augmented reality and game systems

On the technology side, I build augmented reality applications on Apple’s VisionOS stack, focusing on full-scene reconstruction, physically motivated shaders, and shared-world gameplay.

Ongoing work includes an AR role-playing game with MMO-style interaction and real-world environment integration for Apple Vision Pro.

How I work

Across finance, physics, and software, I try to keep the same principles: clear assumptions, explicit tests, and a bias toward concrete results. I am comfortable working with sponsors and operating teams on one side, and with experimentalists and theorists on the other.
