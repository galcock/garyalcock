Research — Density Field Dynamics

Density Field Dynamics is a scalar-field reformulation of gravity and optics in which a single field, psi, lives on flat three-dimensional space and controls both how light moves and how matter accelerates.

  • Optics: the refractive index is n = exp(ψ), so the local one-way speed of light is c multiplied by exp(−ψ).
  • Dynamics: test-mass acceleration is a = (c² / 2) times the gradient of ψ.

In the weak-field limit the framework matches standard post-Newtonian tests but predicts sector-resolved violations of Local Position Invariance, especially in cavity–atom and atom–atom clock comparisons. It also produces simple numerical relations linking the MOND acceleration scale, the fine-structure constant, and the Hubble parameter.
