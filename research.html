Physics & Density Field Dynamics

Independent research program in scalar-refractive gravity and precision metrology.

Density Field Dynamics (DFD) is a scalar-tensor framework in which a refractive field ψ sets both the optical index and test-mass acceleration on flat ℝ³. The emphasis is on quantitative, laboratory-accessible predictions that can be checked with state-of-the-art clocks, interferometers, and astrophysical observations.

Primary Resources

  • DFD main site: densityfielddynamics.com — overview, technical papers, figures, and simulation tools.
  • Zenodo record set: curated DFD-related deposits on Zenodo .

Focus Areas

  • Optical clock tests of Local Position Invariance using cavity–atom ratios.
  • Galaxy-scale phenomenology: rotation curves, acceleration scales, and dark-matter alternatives.
  • Strong-field behavior, gravitational waves, and black-hole observables.
  • Matter-wave interferometry and quantum foundations in a refractive-gravity setting.

For detailed derivations, datasets, and experimental proposals, please refer to densityfielddynamics.com.
